The Tokyo High Road

This tour is packed with places which represent the essential Tokyo. We do not simply go from site to site; our planned route visits each of the Shibuya ward’s most famous areas in a clear but not straight path, which makes you feel like you are really exploring Tokyo both on the main and back streets.

Instead of just walking and looking at each attraction as an outsider, we will enter each location as a new step of our adventure. Naoki and Kevin will take one step further to separate ourselves from other tours; you’re not just another customer to us.

Tokyo is a huge and complicated city. You can’t learn everything about Tokyo’s appeal and beauty just from looking at a guidebook.

We want to introduce the real, local Tokyo even in the middle of the most well-known tourist sites.

Let’s go together to those deep parts only the real Tokyo people know.

After going on this tour, you can really say ‘I came to Tokyo!’ with confidence.

DESTINATIONS

Takeshita Street

The place of origin for Japan's latest "kawaii bunka" (cute fashion) trends, Takeshita Street is the heart of Harajuku. While typically thought of a hub for young people's fashion shopping, it is also lined with many interesting stores with a unique character only found here. Always a prime spot for people watching, you'll be amazed at the variety of strange sights and characters.

Ura Harajuku

Behind the bustling Takeshita Street you'll find an area that mixes the sophisticated edge of nearby Omotesando with the youth and experimentalism of Takeshita Street. The Festa Art Gallery and Cafe is a work of art in itself, and a haven for young artists who display their work for free. The area even features a mega-sized work by famous British street artist Banksy.

Omotesando

Near the end of Takeshita Street we'll find the entrance to a different shopping atmosphere, not one of youthful experiment but one of mature sophistication. Chanel, Louis Vuitton, Gucci - high-end brands from all over the world line the streets. While a go-to shopping area for Tokyo high society, it is also loves by tourists from around the world and thus holds its own as an international site.

Cat Street

Compared to the bright and crowded Takeshita Street, Cat Street is a cool and tasteful area aimed not at teenagers but at yuppies who enjoy a hipster style not found on either Takeshita nor Omotesando street. With its own share of unique stores and cute cafés, Cat Street offers a taste of local flavor.

Shibuya

You wouldn't be wrong to say the Shibuya Crossing is one of the most visited tour spots in Tokyo, if not the most. Every day, people are overwhelmed by the sheer number of pedestrians, with over 500,000 per day, a Guinness world record. However, we will also lead you to the parts of Shibuya almost none of the tourists know, which only the real residents venture to.

Local Café

Far from the chain stores to be found in any major city, Tokyo also boasts a strong coffee culture not to be diminished by Japan's long history of tea drinking. The baristers at our selected shop select only the best roasts and serve them with expertise. You'll be able to take a refreshed step back into the busy Tokyo streets.
