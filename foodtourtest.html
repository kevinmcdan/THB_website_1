Local flavor of Nakano

In this tour, we will sample three essential types of Tokyo cuisine at super local restaurants, enjoy authentic sake at old-fashioned bars, and learn about the traditions of Japanese food and drink culture all in one trip.

Our destination is Nakano, near Shinjuku but far from the crowds. Here, where the sake flows, the sake lovers are sure to go. For people who enjoy authentic sake, Nakano earns five stars every time.

A friendly locale great for first-time visitors, with elements of 1950’s and 60’s Tokyo remaining, Nakano is sure to give the full experience of the real “Local Tokyo”.

If you’re going out for dinner and drinks tonight, Nakano’s your best bet!

DESTINATIONS

Nakano Street

These old-style streets are lined with traditional izakayas. Here, any small nook and cranny bar you enter is sure to be overflowing with sake lovers.

Nigi Nigi Sushi

A unique standing sushi experience not even available in Tsukiji, with sushi cut fresh for your order; a mysterious small booth hidden behind a screen, regular tourists can't find the flavor and wasabi kick waiting here.

Beer Factory

A variety of original Japanese craft beers are available in this treehouse hidden off to the side of Nakano's izakaya-lined streets. While most Japanese beers are lagers, here you can find pale ales, porters, and even a delicious non-alcoholic homemade apple cider.

Yakitori

When westerners think chicken, they tend to think only of wing or breast meat; this is far from the limit of the Japanese yakitori, the grilled chicken skewers often seen as a matter of course for after-work drinking. Upon grilling, you'll find that the savory stomach, liver and even heart of the chicken are not to be wasted, but instead enjoyed as a main course.

Okonomiyaki

Okonomiyaki is a savory pancake made from meat, noodles, cabbage and a thick secret sauce. This Okonomiyaki master is number one in the world, and he knows it. This chance to see Okonomiyaki and yakisoba prepared before your eyes is one you shouldn't miss.

Standing Sake

Nakano's tachinomiya (standing bars) collect a variety of high-quality sake from all around Japan, each with their own regional style, for your drinking pleasure. While tasting the sake, we will teach you about the origin of its flavor, detailing production from the rice all the way to the glass.
